J.R.R. Tolkien

The Mastermind behind The Lord of the Rings

The Timeline of Events of J.R.R. Tolkien's life and Academic Career:

  • 1892 - J.R.R. Tolkien is born in South Africa.
  • 1911 - Tolkien begins to study at Oxford college.
  • 1914 - Tolkien writes his first piece of Middle Earth literature, 'The Voyage of Earendel the Evening Star'.
  • 1916 - Tolkien marries his wife, Edith, in Warwick.
  • 1922 - A Middle English Vocabulary is published by Tolkien, and he begins work with E.V. Gordon on a new edition of Sir Gawain and the Green Knight
  • 1930 - During 1930, it is believed Tolkien may have written the first sentence of The Hobbit: "In a hole in the ground there lived a hobbit".
  • 1933 - The Inklings meetings under the auspices of C.S. Lewis become an important part of Tolkien's social life.
  • 1937 - The Hobbit is published. Because it was tremendously successful, publisher Stanley Unwin pressured Tolkien to write a sequel, which became The Lord of the Rings.
  • 1945 - Tolkien becomes Oxford's Merton Professor of English Language and Literature.
  • 1947 - On Fairy-Stories is published in Essays Presented to Charles Williams.
  • 1949 - Farmer Giles of Ham is published.
  • 1953 - Publication of The Homecoming of Beorhtnoth Beorhthelm's Son.
  • 1954 - Publication of The Fellowship of the Ring.
  • 1954 - Publication of The Two Towers.
  • 1955 - Publication of The Return of the King.
  • Beyond - Because of his success with the Middle Earth series, Tolkien became world reknowned as an author and lived to enjoy his older days. His wife Edith died in 1971, and Tolkien followed soon after in 1973.
